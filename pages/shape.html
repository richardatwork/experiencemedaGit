  • What we shape.

    How does your customers’ experience of your business measure up against their expectations of you?

    How close is their perception of you to the reality?

    If you were to put yourself in their shoes, would you see a gap?

    Because for all the effort that goes into grabbing their attention, there’s often an enormous disparity between the expectation your public brings and when you finally get to meet them in person. A gap that, when a customer comes calling, can undermine a good deal of the effort (not to mention the cost) you invested in getting them there.

    Our experience is that many businesses are organised in such a way that it’s inevitable they work against the delivery of a seamless experience for their customers. The very people responsible for delivering one over-arching, integrated and aligned experience often find themselves working within individual business silos on strategies generally driven from the company's need’s out, rather than from the ‘outside, in’.

    Shaping customer experience is a big challenge. But there is a simple place to start and where we always begin helping organisations make sense of the shape they want be in.

    From you customer’s point of view, there are always fundamental things that need to be ‘got right’ no matter what. They may be small and seemingly insignificant to the business but can make the difference between ‘sold’ and ‘not sold’ to them. Understanding what these Journey Point are becomes the basis of your Experience Strategy.

    At Meda, shaping customer experience - the types of experience that give great returns and get customer’s returning again and again - is a determined through Experience Strategy. It’s the practice by which you can define and deliver your desired customer experience. It becomes your map to navigate by, moving you from your current position to the point where you want your experience to be; your means by which to achieve, not just the efficient, but the effective alignment of your places, messages, people and processes.

    Ultimately it will enable you to deliver an experience that’s unique and directly attributable to your business alone. Qualities that can be owned by you and most importantly, ones that will turn a prospect into a loyal advocate. Transforming your ambition into action and closing the gap.
